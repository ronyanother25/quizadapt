Kinematics
P1.Level 1
A ball is thrown vertically upward with initial velocity 20 m/s. The maximum height reached is (g = 10 m/s²):
(A) 10 m
(B) 20 m
(C) 30 m
(D) 40 m
Hint 1: At max height, final velocity v = 0.Hint 2: Use v² = u² − 2gH → 0 = 400 − 20H.Hint 3: H = 400/20 = 20 m.
P2.Level 2
A projectile is fired at 60° above horizontal with speed 40 m/s. Time of flight is (g = 10 m/s²):
(A) 4 s
(B) 4√2 s
(C) 4√3 s
(D) 8 s
Hint 1: T = 2u sinθ / g.Hint 2: sin 60° = √3/2; T = 2 × 40 × (√3/2) / 10.Hint 3: T = 80√3/20 = 4√3 s ≈ 6.93 s.
P3.Level 2
Position of a particle: x = 2t² + 3t + 1 (in metres). Velocity at t = 2 s is:
(A) 7 m/s
(B) 9 m/s
(C) 11 m/s
(D) 13 m/s
Hint 1: Differentiate x with respect to t: v = dx/dt.Hint 2: v = 4t + 3.Hint 3: At t = 2: v = 8 + 3 = 11 m/s.
P4.Level 2
A body thrown at 30° above horizontal at 20 m/s. Horizontal range (g = 10 m/s²):
(A) 20 m
(B) 20√3 m
(C) 40 m
(D) 40√3 m
Hint 1: R = u² sin 2θ / g.Hint 2: sin 60° = √3/2; R = 400 × (√3/2) / 10.Hint 3: R = 200√3/10 = 20√3 m.
P5.Level 3
Position vector: r⃗ = (3t²î + 2tĵ) m. Magnitude of acceleration at t = 2 s is:
(A) 2 m/s²
(B) 4 m/s²
(C) 6 m/s²
(D) 8 m/s²
Hint 1: v⃗ = dr⃗/dt = (6tî + 2ĵ).Hint 2: a⃗ = dv⃗/dt = 6î (constant).Hint 3: |a⃗| = 6 m/s² (independent of t).
Laws of Motion
P6.Level 1
A 10 kg block on a frictionless surface is acted on by a 40 N horizontal force. Acceleration is:
(A) 2 m/s²
(B) 3 m/s²
(C) 4 m/s²
(D) 5 m/s²
Hint 1: Newton's second law: F = ma.Hint 2: a = F/m = 40/10.Hint 3: a = 4 m/s².
P7.Level 2
Block A (5 kg) on a surface (μ = 0.2) is connected via string over frictionless pulley to hanging block B (5 kg). Acceleration of system (g = 10 m/s²):
(A) 2 m/s²
(B) 3 m/s²
(C) 4 m/s²
(D) 5 m/s²
Hint 1: Net force = weight of B − friction on A.Hint 2: Net F = 5×10 − 0.2×5×10 = 50 − 10 = 40 N.Hint 3: a = 40/(5+5) = 4 m/s².
P8.Level 2
A man of mass 80 kg stands on a scale in a lift accelerating upward at 2 m/s². Scale reading (g = 10 m/s²):
(A) 640 N
(B) 800 N
(C) 960 N
(D) 1120 N
Hint 1: Apparent weight = m(g + a).Hint 2: R = 80 × (10 + 2).Hint 3: R = 80 × 12 = 960 N.
P9.Level 2
Two blocks of 2 kg and 3 kg on a frictionless surface are connected by a string. A 10 N force acts on the 2 kg block. Tension in the string is:
(A) 2 N
(B) 4 N
(C) 6 N
(D) 8 N
Hint 1: System acceleration: a = 10/(2+3) = 2 m/s².Hint 2: Tension accelerates only the 3 kg block.Hint 3: T = 3 × 2 = 6 N.
P10.Level 3
A block is in equilibrium on a rough incline (30°, μ = 0.5√3). Minimum force parallel to incline needed to prevent sliding down (g = 10 m/s², mass = 2 kg):
(A) 0 N (no force needed)
(B) 2 N down the incline
(C) 5 N up the incline
(D) 10 N
Hint 1: Check if block slides: compare mg sinθ with μmg cosθ.Hint 2: mg sin30° = 10 N; μmg cos30° = 0.5√3×2×10×(√3/2) = 15 N.Hint 3: Friction (15 N) > gravity component (10 N), so block stays. No external force needed.
Work, Energy & Power
P11.Level 1
A 5 kg block is pushed 3 m along a surface by a 20 N force at 60° to horizontal. Work done by the force:
(A) 20 J
(B) 30 J
(C) 40 J
(D) 60 J
Hint 1: W = F · d · cosθ.Hint 2: W = 20 × 3 × cos60°.Hint 3: cos60° = 0.5; W = 60 × 0.5 = 30 J.
P12.Level 2
A spring (k = 1000 N/m) is compressed by 0.1 m. Elastic potential energy stored:
(A) 0.5 J
(B) 5 J
(C) 10 J
(D) 50 J
Hint 1: PE = ½kx².Hint 2: PE = ½ × 1000 × (0.1)².Hint 3: PE = 500 × 0.01 = 5 J.
P13.Level 2
A 5 kg body at 6 m/s collides and sticks to a stationary 3 kg body (perfectly inelastic). Final speed:
(A) 3 m/s
(B) 3.75 m/s
(C) 4 m/s
(D) 5 m/s
Hint 1: Conservation of momentum: m₁u₁ = (m₁+m₂)v.Hint 2: 5×6 = (5+3)v → 30 = 8v.Hint 3: v = 30/8 = 3.75 m/s.
P14.Level 2
Work done by gravity on a 2 kg ball falling freely through 10 m (g = 10 m/s²):
(A) 100 J
(B) 150 J
(C) 200 J
(D) 250 J
Hint 1: Work by gravity = mgh (force and displacement in same direction).Hint 2: W = 2 × 10 × 10.Hint 3: W = 200 J.
P15.Level 3
A particle moves from (0,0) to (2,4) in force field F⃗ = (3x²î + 4yĵ) N. Work done:
(A) 24 J
(B) 32 J
(C) 40 J
(D) 48 J
Hint 1: W = ∫F⃗·dr⃗ = ∫3x²dx + ∫4ydy (this force is conservative).Hint 2: ∫₀²3x²dx = [x³]₀² = 8 J; ∫₀⁴4ydy = [2y²]₀⁴ = 32 J.Hint 3: W = 8 + 32 = 40 J.

Energy of an electron in the 2nd Bohr orbit of hydrogen atom:
(A) −13.6 eV

(D) −0.85 eV
Hint 1: Eₙ = −13.6/n² eV for hydrogen.Hint 2: n = 2 → E₂ = −13.6/4.Hint 3: E₂ = −3.4 eV.
C2.Level 1
Number of radial nodes in a 3p orbital:
(A) 0
(B) 1
(C) 2
(D) 3
Hint 1: Radial nodes = n − l − 1.Hint 2: For 3p: n=3, l=1.Hint 3: 3 − 1 − 1 = 1 radial node.
C3.Level 2
In Bohr's model, ratio of time periods T₂/T₁ for electron in hydrogen atom:
(A) 2
(B) 4
(C) 6
(D) 8
Hint 1: Tₙ ∝ n³/Z² (for same element, Z is constant).Hint 2: T₂/T₁ = (2/1)³.Hint 3: T₂/T₁ = 8.
C4.Level 2
de Broglie wavelength associated with electron in 3rd Bohr orbit of H (a₀ = 0.529 Å):
(A) 3.3 Å
(B) 6.6 Å
(C) 9.9 Å
(D) 13.3 Å
Hint 1: Bohr condition: 2πrₙ = nλ → λ = 2πrₙ/n.Hint 2: r₃ = 9a₀ = 9×0.529 = 4.76 Å; λ = 2π×4.76/3.Hint 3: λ = 29.9/3 ≈ 9.97 ≈ 9.9 Å (one full orbit circumference divided by n).
C5.Level 3
Minimum uncertainty in velocity of electron (m = 9.1×10⁻³¹ kg) if position uncertainty is 0.1 Å (h = 6.63×10⁻³⁴ J·s):
(A) 5.8×10⁵ m/s
(B) 5.8×10⁶ m/s
(C) 5.8×10⁷ m/s
(D) 5.8×10⁴ m/s
Hint 1: Heisenberg: Δx·Δp ≥ h/4π → Δv ≥ h/(4π·m·Δx).Hint 2: Δv = 6.63×10⁻³⁴ / (4π × 9.1×10⁻³¹ × 10⁻¹¹).Hint 3: Δv ≈ 6.63×10⁻³⁴ / (1.14×10⁻⁴⁰) ≈ 5.8×10⁶ m/s.

(A) C≡C < C=C < C−C

Thermodynamics
C11.Level 1
Standard enthalpy of formation of an element in its most stable standard state is:
(A) +1 kJ/mol
(B) 0 kJ/mol
(C) −1 kJ/mol
(D) Depends on the element
Hint 1: Standard enthalpy of formation is defined for the formation of 1 mol of a substance from elements in their standard states.Hint 2: An element in its standard state forms itself — no reaction occurs.Hint 3: ΔH°f = 0 for all elements in their standard states (e.g., O₂(g), C(graphite)).
C12.Level 2
For CO(g) + ½O₂(g) → CO₂(g), the value of Δn_gas is:
(A) +0.5
(B) −0.5
(C) +1
(D) −1
Hint 1: Δn = moles of gaseous products − moles of gaseous reactants.Hint 2: Products: 1 mol CO₂; Reactants: 1 mol CO + 0.5 mol O₂ = 1.5 mol.Hint 3: Δn = 1 − 1.5 = −0.5.
C13.Level 2
For a spontaneous process at constant T and P, the Gibbs free energy change ΔG is:
(A) ΔG > 0
(B) ΔG = 0
(C) ΔG < 0
(D) ΔG ≥ 0
Hint 1: ΔG = ΔH − TΔS is the criterion for spontaneity at constant T, P.Hint 2: System moves to lower free energy spontaneously.Hint 3: Spontaneous process: ΔG < 0; equilibrium: ΔG = 0; non-spontaneous: ΔG > 0.
C14.Level 2
ΔH_vap of water = 40.7 kJ/mol at 100°C (373 K). Entropy change during vaporisation:
(A) 55 J/mol·K
(B) 80 J/mol·K
(C) 109 J/mol·K
(D) 137 J/mol·K
Hint 1: At equilibrium (boiling point): ΔG = 0 → ΔS = ΔH/T.Hint 2: ΔS = 40700 J/mol ÷ 373 K.Hint 3: ΔS ≈ 109 J/mol·K.
C15.Level 3
2 mol of ideal gas at 300 K expands reversibly and isothermally from 1 L to 10 L. Work done by gas (R = 8.314 J/mol·K):
(A) −5.74 kJ
(B) −11.48 kJ
(C) +11.48 kJ
(D) −22.96 kJ
Hint 1: W = −nRT ln(V₂/V₁) (work done on gas; by gas is opposite sign).Hint 2: W_by gas = nRT ln(V₂/V₁) = 2 × 8.314 × 300 × ln10.Hint 3: = 4988 × 2.303 ≈ 11480 J ≈ 11.48 kJ (positive, gas expands). W = −11.48 kJ done on gas → W_by gas = +11.48 kJ; answer is −11.48 kJ for work done on gas.

C17.Level 2
EMF of the cell Zn|Zn²⁺||Cu²⁺|Cu. Given E°(Zn²⁺/Zn) = −0.76 V, E°(Cu²⁺/Cu) = +0.34 V:

C18.Level 2
Molar conductivity of a weak electrolyte at infinite dilution is best determined by:
(A) Direct measurement at infinite dilution

C19.Level 2
Time required to deposit 0.54 g of silver (At. wt. = 108) by electrolysis using 0.5 A current (F = 96500 C/mol):
(A) 482.5 s
(B) 965 s

C20.Level 3
EMF of cell Zn|Zn²⁺(0.1 M)||Cu²⁺(0.01 M)|Cu at 25°C (E°cell = 1.10 V):
(A) 1.04 V
(B) 1.07 V

A primary amine treated with NaNO₂ + HCl at 0–5°C gives:
(A) Secondary amine

C25.Level 3
Which of these is the correct decreasing order of reactivity of halides in nucleophilic substitution (SN2)?
(A) RF > RCl > RBr > RI
(B) RI > RBr > RCl > RF
(C) RCl > RBr > RI > RF
(D) RF > RBr > RCl > RI
Hint 1: SN2 rate depends on C−X bond strength (leaving group ability).Hint 2: Better leaving group = weaker base = weaker conjugate acid of leaving group's anion.Hint 3: I⁻ is the best leaving group (weakest base); F⁻ is the worst. RI > RBr > RCl > RF.

Limits & Continuity
M1.Level 1
lim(x→0) [sin 3x / 5x] =
(A) 0
(B) 1
(C) 3/5
(D) 5/3
Hint 1: Use standard result: lim(θ→0) sinθ/θ = 1.Hint 2: Write sin3x/5x = (3/5) × (sin3x/3x).Hint 3: As x→0, sin3x/3x → 1; so limit = 3/5.
M2.Level 2
lim(x→0) [(1 − cos x) / x²] =
(A) 0
(B) 1/2
(C) 1
(D) Does not exist
Hint 1: Use half-angle: 1 − cosx = 2sin²(x/2).Hint 2: (1−cosx)/x² = 2sin²(x/2)/x² = (1/2)[sin(x/2)/(x/2)]².Hint 3: As x→0, [sin(x/2)/(x/2)]² → 1, so limit = 1/2.
M3.Level 2
f(x) = |x − 2| is not differentiable at:
(A) x = 0
(B) x = 1
(C) x = 2
(D) x = 3
Hint 1: |x − 2| has a sharp corner where x − 2 = 0.Hint 2: Left derivative at x=2: −1; Right derivative: +1.Hint 3: Since L.H.D ≠ R.H.D at x = 2, not differentiable there.
M4.Level 2
lim(x→1) [(x² − 1) / (x − 1)] =
(A) 0
(B) 1
(C) 2
(D) Does not exist
Hint 1: Factor the numerator: x² − 1 = (x+1)(x−1).Hint 2: Cancel (x−1): limit = lim(x→1) (x+1).Hint 3: = 1 + 1 = 2.
M5.Level 3
f(x) = x·sin(1/x) for x ≠ 0 and f(0) = 0. At x = 0, f is:
(A) Continuous and differentiable
(B) Continuous but not differentiable
(C) Differentiable but not continuous
(D) Neither continuous nor differentiable
Hint 1: Check continuity: |x·sin(1/x)| ≤ |x| → 0 as x→0. So f is continuous.Hint 2: Check differentiability: f'(0) = lim(h→0) h·sin(1/h)/h = lim sin(1/h).Hint 3: lim sin(1/h) oscillates between −1 and 1 — limit doesn't exist. Not differentiable.

M8.Level 2
∫ x·eˣ dx =
(A) x·eˣ + C
(B) eˣ + C
(C) (x+1)eˣ + C
(D) (x−1)eˣ + C
Hint 1: Use integration by parts: ∫u dv = uv − ∫v du.Hint 2: Let u = x, dv = eˣdx → du = dx, v = eˣ.Hint 3: = x·eˣ − ∫eˣdx = x·eˣ − eˣ + C = (x−1)eˣ + C.
M9.Level 2

(D) 2(ln 2)/3
Hint 1: Substitution: let u = 1 + x³, du = 3x² dx.Hint 2: Limits change: x=0 → u=1; x=1 → u=2.Hint 3: (1/3)∫₁² du/u = (1/3)[ln u]₁² = (ln 2)/3.
M10.Level 3
Area enclosed between y = x² and y = x (in square units):
(A) 1/3
(B) 1/4
(C) 1/6
(D) 1/12
Hint 1: Find intersection: x² = x → x = 0 or x = 1. On [0,1], x > x².Hint 2: Area = ∫₀¹ (x − x²) dx = [x²/2 − x³/3]₀¹.Hint 3: = 1/2 − 1/3 = 3/6 − 2/6 = 1/6.
